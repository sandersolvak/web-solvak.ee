The Guardian, a newspaper, has bravely published an article on how news is actually bad for us. Not investigative journalism, but the fast sensationalist media bombarding us with “bite-sized” bullets of news that’s designed to interrupt and mislead and in the end leave us desensitized and in chronic stress

Libre Franklin Light at 16pt. Folders are hard. They’re hard in the sense of the mental burden of organising information into deep hierarchies. Coming up with a logical structure and navigating it later takes mental energy. Much like the simple fact of having gears on your bike reserves some of your brain to constantly think about them. You may not notice it and if someone tried to tell you it’s so, you might even disagree. It’s such a small and unnoticeable thing. But these things add up, and you really should use your brain for more important stuff.

Folders are also hard in the sense of being unconditional — you’re taking some stuff, putting it into a drawer and closing it. It’s hiding things from view, making you remember where, in which drawer that thing you’re looking for is.
